## Free isn’t the problem. Aimless is.

Free plans can be power tools or money pits. The difference is whether the path to paid is obvious, fair, and easy.

Let’s make it obvious.

## Start with the outcome, not the paywall

Define the job you help them finish. Put your best outcome in the free plan—then cap scale, collaboration, or speed.

- Docs: unlimited personal docs, 3 collaborators max → paid removes the cap
- Analytics: 1 dashboard, 1 data source → paid adds sources and alerts
- Automation: 1 live workflow → paid increases runs and parallelism

## Design the “why now?” moments

- In-product prompts tied to success: after they hit the first outcome, offer the next step (which is paid)
- Usage-based nudges: “You’re at 80% of your free limit. Here’s what paid unlocks.”
- Time-gated features: a 7-day taste of a premium feature right when it makes sense

Keep it kind, clear, and reversible. No traps.

## Make paying feel like a continuation, not a switch

- Same UI, just more power — don’t move their cheese
- One-click upgrades; keep context (no detours to a maze-like pricing page)
- Annual plans only after they’ve felt the value for a bit (you’ll thank yourself later)

## What to say (words matter)

- “Unlock X for your team” beats “Upgrade to Pro.” It’s about them, not your plan names
- “Add 3 more collaborators” is clearer than “Pro has team features”
- Show outcomes: “Get weekly email reports” > “Advanced reporting”

## A simple funnel to track

- Free signups → activated (hit the first outcome)
- Activated → hit a limit (or success milestone)
- Hit limit → viewed paywall
- Viewed paywall → paid

Plug holes where the drop-offs are worst. One at a time.

## Two experiments this week

- Add a soft wall on the feature people touch right after activation. Tie it to a meaningful upgrade.
- Replace “Upgrade” CTAs with outcome-labeled CTAs on the top two surfaces.
